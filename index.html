Understanding Your Carbon Footprint

5 min read · Transport, Food, Energy

💡 Key takeaway: The average person in the EU produces 8.4 tonnes of CO₂ equivalent per year. Three areas — transport, food, and home energy — account for over 70% of that.

What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gas emissions caused directly and indirectly by an individual, organisation, event or product. It is expressed in tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent (CO₂e), which accounts for all gases — methane, nitrous oxide, and others — scaled to their warming potential.

The three biggest sources

🚗 Transport (29%) — Cars are the biggest single contributor. A petrol car emits ~171g CO₂ per km. Switching to public transport or cycling cuts this dramatically.
🍔 Food (26%) — Beef is the most carbon-intensive food. Replacing one beef meal per week with plant-based food saves ~340 kg CO₂ annually.
🏠 Home Energy (21%) — Heating and cooling dominate. Lowering your thermostat by 1°C saves around 8% of heating energy.

About EcoTrack

We believe small everyday choices add up to massive global change. EcoTrack makes sustainable living easy, measurable, and rewarding for everyone.

♿

Designed for Everyone

EcoTrack meets WCAG 2.1 AA standards — keyboard navigable, screen-reader friendly, and high-contrast accessible.

Our Story

Born from a passion for the planet

EcoTrack started as a university project at Vilnius University Šiauliai Academy, created as part of a Human-Computer Interaction assignment. The challenge was to build something real — a tool that actually helps people live more sustainably, not just looks good on paper.

What began as an academic exercise became a genuine passion project. Every screen, every button, every piece of feedback in this app was designed with one question in mind: "Does this make it easier for a real person to make a greener choice today?"
